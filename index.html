Step 1. Understanding the Problem Statement

Sesame is a patient-centered healthcare startup focused on connecting patients to affordable care. A key part of its digital platform is a web-based medical content (blogs and videos) hub where patients can access high-quality health information. The target users are cost-conscious patients seeking credible provider options and providers looking to attract visits through the platform. Currently, Sesame aims to address three main design issues:

  • For patients: increase the readability and accessibility of medical content
  • For providers: improve the content creation experience through AI assistance and account growth tracking
  • For both: create effective channels that convert patients into provider visits

The ultimate goal is to deliver clear and trustworthy health information that guides patients to care options, while boosting provider engagement and visibility. It is hence important that our design satisfies the needs of both groups.

Next, we can combine these sketches to create wireframes using the Balsamiq software. We created a initial set of 4 wireframes. Under each one, you can read explanations about how it addresses a problem stated previously and impacts user experience.

Page 1: Blog Homepage

  • Although our original sketches didn't include a blog homepage, during team discussions we realized that adding one would help users discover topic-based content and browse articles based on popularity. This enhancement supports increased user engagement and boosts conversion rates.
  • Our design moves away from the original text-heavy blog and creates a modular, user-focused experience.
  • At the top, the hero section now features a welcome message alongside a search bar, helping users quickly find relevant health content. This upgrade improves information accessibility and shifts the experience from passive reading to active exploration and interaction.

Page 2: Individual Article Page

  • We used the design from Set 1 as the baseline, as its layout is the most intuitive and offers a strong information hierarchy.
  • We also incorporated a floating section featuring the provider from Set 2. Users can click into the provider's profile, book an appointment, or ask questions directly. By improving the provider's visibility, we aim to increase conversion rates and help build the provider's personal brand, which is aligned with Sesame's goal.
  • In addition, we added the video section from Set 4 to address the issue of long text and improve article readability‚Äîespecially for users who prefer video content. While this feature may require more effort from providers, our built-in AI tool can streamline the process by converting articles into video scripts.

Page 3: Provider Dashboard

  • We combined the designs from Sets 1, 3, and 4. Specifically, we used Set 4 as the baseline and integrated key features from Sets 1 and 3.
  • The most important feature is the Quick Stats section, which provides providers with an immediate overview of their content's performance‚Äîseparated by articles and videos. By displaying metrics such as total reads, conversion rates, and likes, we reinforce the tangible value of their contributions. This aligns with Sesame's goal of encouraging providers to consistently generate content by making performance data easily accessible and actionable.
  • At the top right, the "Create Content" call-to-action is designed to encourage immediate content creation. Clicking this button leads to an AI-powered editor, reducing the time needed to publish high-quality content. This feature supports Sesame's mission to empower providers to produce scalable and authentic content efficiently.

Page 4: AI Assistant Page

  • Our team debated between two approaches. The first was a step-by-step, modularized AI assistant. This approach makes it easy for providers to draft an article, as the steps are built-in and clearly defined. However, it also greatly limits what a provider can do. The second approach was a chat-based AI assistant. This offers providers more freedom but is more open-ended, and users might not engage with it as we intend.
  • After analyzing the trade-offs, we decided to go with the chat-based AI assistant, considering users' conceptual models regarding AI assistants‚Äîthey may already be familiar with this format through prior experience with tools like ChatGPT and Claude.
  • We also created an interactive canvas for user editing (inspired by set 2), where users can highlight sentences they want to refine and prompt the AI to revise them.
  • After some discussion, we decided not to include a blog generation feature in order to preserve providers' autonomy in content creation and to prevent potential misinformation or errors in AI-generated content.

Step 3. First Round of Feedback from Startup

After presenting our low-fidelity wireframes to Sesame‚Äôs product team, we received thoughtful and encouraging feedback from Ravi Mehta and Chris Chon. Below is a summary of the major themes from the feedback:

  • Modular Hero Section: The team appreciated the simplicity and modularity of the homepage layout. Chris expressed interest in seeing stronger visual treatments in the HiFi version.
  • Search Experience: Ravi noted that users are often overwhelmed by traditional search and struggle with medical terminology. Both Ravi and Chris encouraged us to design a more assistive and exploratory search feature to guide passive users toward active discovery.
  • Visual Design Consistency: Feedback included ensuring consistency in thumbnails and style for articles, especially as content scales. Ravi asked for design guidelines for choosing images.
  • Content Organization: Ravi suggested refining the taxonomy of topics and determining which categories are best to feature. Chris asked how headers like "Featured" and "Not sure where to start" were chosen and encouraged research-based justification.
  • Fact-Checking and CTA Design: Chris encouraged us to prototype what fact-checking and interactive CTAs (like "ask me questions") would look like in HiFi.
  • Dashboard and Stats: Chris and Ravi were excited about the AI insight section and asked whether other metrics might better incentivize providers to create content. Suggestions were made for personalization and recommendation features for logged-in users.

This feedback helped us prioritize clarity, interactivity, and scalable design systems in our HiFi iterations.

Step 4. Hi-Fi Prototyping

Below are the final high-fidelity prototypes for five redesigned screens. Each layout emphasizes usability, responsiveness, and visual hierarchy while supporting Sesame‚Äôs business goals.

Page 1: Blog Homepage

  • Assistive Exploration: Introduced a bold hero section with a search bar and helpful query prompts to guide users toward active content discovery.
  • Visual Hierarchy: Enhanced the Featured and Recent Articles with large thumbnails and clear overlays to highlight high-value content.
  • User Onboarding: Added a "Not Sure Where to Get Started?" section with topic-based navigation to support first-time or uncertain users.

Page 2: Individual Article Page

  • Seamless Conversion: Integrated provider profile with direct "Book Appointment" and "Ask Questions" CTAs to streamline patient action-taking.
  • Improved Readability: Article content is structured with headers, callout boxes, and bulleted lists to support scanning and comprehension.
  • Assistive Multimedia: Optional embedded video in the hero section offers an alternative to text-heavy content for different user preferences.

Page 3: Provider Dashboard

  • Quick Stats Panel: Displays article count, reads, bookings, and conversion rate with rank context, allowing providers to track growth at a glance.
  • Smart Suggestions: Highlights trending topics and suggested content creation areas based on user interest and platform trends.
  • Actionable Interface: Clear CTAs for ‚ÄúCreate Articles,‚Äù ‚ÄúUse Drafty AI,‚Äù and ‚ÄúView Performance‚Äù encourage frequent publishing and experimentation.

Page 4: AI Assistant Page

  • Conversational Interface: Providers interact with Drafty in a familiar chat-based flow, making the drafting process intuitive and flexible.
  • Interactive Canvas: Users can highlight and directly edit content, with options to prompt Drafty for rewrites, style changes, or fact-checking within context.
  • Multi-modal Export: Finalized text articles are automatically converted into downloadable scripts for video generation, streamlining cross-format content creation.

Page 5: Appointment Booking Flow

  • Inline Scheduling: Patients can book directly from within article or provider views, streamlining the journey from discovery to conversion.
  • Modal Interface: A pop-up modal keeps users anchored to the content while booking, improving retention and reducing drop-off.
  • Multi-provider Support: Patients can browse available times across providers without toggling between multiple pages.

Step 5. Final Feedback and Reflection

After delivering our high-fidelity prototype, we conducted another review session with the Sesame team. They appreciated the clearer conversion pathways and praised the AI assistant‚Äôs modularity and editing experience. Our changes addressed the key feedback points raised in Step 3 and aligned closely with Sesame‚Äôs strategic vision.

If we had more time, we would run usability tests on mobile responsiveness, A/B test the blog layout vs. provider-first layout, and gather analytics on engagement with Drafty AI. Overall, this project taught us the value of iterative user feedback and how collaborative design can support both patient and provider needs in real-world applications.
